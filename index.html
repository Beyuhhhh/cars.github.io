2023 Chevrolet Corvette

The Chevrolet Corvette is a line of American two-door, two-seater luxury sports cars, manufactured and marketed by GM, under the Chevrolet marque, since 1953.

Nissan GT-R R35

Nissan GT-R is powered by the Nissan VR (engine) VR38DETT 3799 cc (3.8 L) DOHC V6. Two parallel turbochargers, Ishikawajima-Harima Heavy Industries (IHI), provide fast supercharging. The engine has a power of 404 kW (550 hp) at 6500 rpm and 632 Nm at 3200-5800 rpm.

Toyota Supra

Toyota introduced the Celica Supra in 1979. Its styling was based upon the popular Celica liftback design, but it was both longer and wider. It was the engine, though, that made the Supra stand apart from the Celica.

McLaren 720S.

According to McLaren the 720S can accelerate to 100 km/h (62 mph) in 2.9 seconds, to 200 km/h (124 mph) in 7.8 seconds, can achieve a maximum speed of 341 km/h (212 mph), and has a 1/4 mile (402 m) time of 10.3 seconds.

Porsche 911

The Porsche 911 (pronounced Nine Eleven or in German: Neunelf) is a two-door 2+2 high performance rear-engined sports car introduced in September 1964 by Porsche AG of Stuttgart, Germany. It has a rear-mounted flat-six engine and originally a torsion bar suspension.

Ford Mustang

The namesake of the "pony car" automobile segment, the Mustang was developed as a highly styled line of sporty coupes and convertibles derived from existing model lines, initially distinguished by "long hood, short deck" proportions.

BMW M4

The BMW M4 Coupé is a puristic sports coupé with outstanding driving characteristics. Responsible for this are a 480-hp high-performance BMW M TwinPower Turbo power unit, rear-wheel drive and highly developed technologies with their origins in motorsport, such as the M specific suspension with Active M Differential.

Honda Civic Type R FL5

Honda Civic Type R, known within the car circles by its internal 'FL5' chassis code. In comparison with the first FK8, the FL5 can be summarized as “two steps back, three steps forward”, largely based on its looks..

Subaru WRX STI

The Subaru WRX STI is a variant of the Subaru WRX sedan produced by in-house tuning company Subaru Tecnica International between 2014 and 2021. It succeeded the Subaru Impreza WRX STI. The VA WRX STI was the last vehicle produced with the famous EJ257 engine.

Toyota 86

The GT86 range was designed for pure driving pleasure. The bold, aerodynamic styling is enhanced by the stylised front bumper, grille and striking LED headlights. A sleek rear bumper with signature LED lights and distinctive 17” alloy wheels give the GT86 the attitude to match its extraordinary ability.
